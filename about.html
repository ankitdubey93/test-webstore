Our Story

A decade of proving that beautiful clothes and an ethical supply chain aren't mutually exclusive — they're inseparable.

The Beginning

Started in a Garage. Built on Principles.

In the spring of 2015, Maya Osei quit her job as a textile buyer for a major fast-fashion chain. She had spent five years watching factory workers in Bangladesh and Cambodia earn poverty wages while producing clothes that fell apart after three washes — and she believed something better was possible.

Maya launched Lumara Apparel from her garage in Portland's Pearl District with $18,000 in personal savings, one secondhand Juki sewing machine, and a collection of three organic cotton T-shirts in five colours. She sold the first 200 units entirely through Instagram and local farmers' markets.

The name "Lumara" is a combination of the Latin lumen (light) and the Ghanaian name Amara (grace) — reflecting Maya's dual heritage and her belief that fashion should illuminate rather than exploit.

🪡
"I wasn't trying to build a brand. I was trying to prove that the way the fashion industry operated wasn't inevitable — it was a choice. And we could choose differently."
Maya Osei

A Decade of Growth

Our Journey

From three T-shirts in a garage to 250+ products in 28 countries — without compromising our values once.

2015

Founded in Portland

Maya Osei launches Lumara with 3 tee styles. First 200 units sold through Instagram and Portland markets. Annual revenue: $47,000.

2016

First Wholesale Partners

Partnered with 12 independent boutiques across the Pacific Northwest. Moved to a 2,000 sq ft studio. Team grew to 8 people.

2017

First Factory — Braga, Portugal

Acquired a former textile mill in Braga and began a 14-month renovation. Hired factory director Ana Carvalho to lead operations.

2018

Portland Flagship Opens

Opened our first retail store in Portland's Pearl District — designed by Studio Osei with 100% reclaimed materials. Revenue surpassed $4M.

2019

GOTS & Fairtrade Certified

After two years of supply-chain preparation, achieved GOTS and Fairtrade Textile Standard certification. Jaipur factory opened.

2020

Pandemic — Zero Lay-offs

Despite a 60% revenue drop during COVID lockdowns, Lumara retained all 180 employees by pivoting to PPE production for local hospitals. No furloughs.

2021

New York SoHo Flagship

Opened the 4,800 sq ft New York flagship — our most ambitious retail space. Revenue reached $22M. 50 product styles.

2022

Cusco Factory & Carbon Pledge

Third factory opened in Cusco, Peru, specialising in Andean wool. Signed the UN Fashion Charter, pledging carbon neutrality by 2028.

2023

European Expansion

London Marylebone and Amsterdam Jordaan stores opened. Rotterdam EU distribution hub launched. Revenue: $38M.

2024

Tokyo Store & Asia Pacific

Tokyo Daikanyama store opened in June. Began shipping to Australia, New Zealand, and South Korea. Revenue surpassed $50M.

2025–26

250+ Products, 675 Employees

Today: 8 stores, 3 factories, 2 distribution centres, 675 employees worldwide, and a relentless pursuit of better — always.

Commitments, Not Promises

Sustainability Goals

We publish our progress openly. These aren't aspirations — they're contractual commitments with third-party verification.

Carbon Neutral by 2028

Signed the UN Fashion Charter for Climate Action. Current Scope 1+2 emissions down 42% since 2020 baseline. All factories on renewable energy. Scope 3 supply-chain roadmap in place.

100% Organic or Recycled Fibres by 2027

Currently 92% of fibres are GOTS organic or GRS-certified recycled. The remaining 8% (specialist functional fibres) will reach certification by Q2 2027.

Living Wage — All Employees

Verified by the Fair Wage Network. All 675 employees globally earn at least 130% of local living wage. Average factory wage in Jaipur: ₹28,400/month vs. ₹17,200 local living wage benchmark.

Zero Waste to Landfill by 2026

Currently at 97% diversion rate. Fabric offcuts → zero-waste accessories line. Defective garments → upcycling partner network. Packaging → 100% recycled or compostable already achieved.

Water Reduction — 50% by 2027

Braga facility already at 94% water recycling. Jaipur pursuing ZLD (Zero Liquid Discharge) by 2027. Cusco uses no dyeing processes — naturally coloured or undyed alpaca fibres only.

Product Longevity Programme

Free in-store repairs for life on any Lumara garment. Resale platform (Lumara Second) launched Q1 2025. Take-back recycling in all 8 stores. Designing for durability (50-wash colour-fastness minimum).
