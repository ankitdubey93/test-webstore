The Lumara Manufacturing Process

We own and operate every stage of our production — which means we can guarantee standards others can only promise.

1

Design & Pattern Making

Each Lumara garment begins in our Portland design studio. Our team of 12 designers works with Clo3D digital pattern software to create and refine silhouettes before a single thread is cut. Every pattern is graded across 7 standard sizes (XS–3XL) with inclusive fit modelling using a diverse panel of 24 fit models.

Design iterations are reviewed for material efficiency — we target less than 8% fabric waste per pattern through strategic nesting algorithms. Approved designs are sent digitally to our factories to eliminate paper-based transfers.

Clo3D Software 12 In-House Designers 7 Size Grades 24 Fit Model Panel <8% Fabric Waste Target
2

Raw Material Sourcing

We source exclusively from certified farms and suppliers. Our primary cotton comes from Fairtrade-certified cooperatives in Gujarat, India, and the Nile Delta, Egypt. Linen fibres originate from BFLF-certified farms in Belgium and France. Wool is sourced from RWS-certified stations in New Zealand and Patagonia, Argentina.

All raw fibres are tested for pesticide residues and heavy metals at our in-house testing lab before acceptance. Suppliers must maintain GOTS or equivalent certification and pass a biannual third-party social audit.

Fairtrade Cotton — Gujarat, India Egyptian Cotton — Nile Delta Belgian Linen — BFLF Certified RWS Wool — New Zealand In-House Pesticide Testing Biannual Social Audits
3

Spinning & Weaving / Knitting

Raw fibres are spun into yarn at our partner mills — Coats & Clark Portugal for ring-spun cotton and Reda SpA in Italy for fine wools. Woven fabrics (denim, canvas, twill) are woven on Dornier rapier looms; knit fabrics (jersey, rib, french terry) are produced on Mayer & Cie circular knitting machines at our Braga factory.

Our Braga facility operates 48 knitting machines running 24/5 (closed Sunday) producing approximately 180,000 metres of fabric per month. All fabric rolls are batch-tagged with QR codes linking to the fibre origin and spinning records.

Dornier Rapier Looms Mayer & Cie Circular Knitters Ring-Spun Cotton Yarn 180,000m / Month Capacity QR Batch Traceability
4

Dyeing & Finishing

All dyeing is performed using OEKO-TEX STANDARD 100 certified reactive dyes and GOTS-approved auxiliaries. Our Braga factory uses a closed-loop dyeing system that recycles 94% of process water through ultrafiltration and reverse-osmosis treatment. Dye effluent is treated to pH 6.5–8.5 before discharge — fully compliant with EU Water Framework Directive.

We do not use azo dyes that can release carcinogenic amines, chlorine bleach, or formaldehyde-based finishes. Fabric finishing includes enzyme washing, sanforizing (pre-shrinking), and calendering for consistent hand-feel across production batches.

OEKO-TEX Reactive Dyes 94% Water Recycling Zero Azo Dyes Enzyme Washing Sanforizing Pre-Shrink EU WFD Compliant
5

Cutting

Fabric cutting is performed using Lectra Investronica computerised spreading and cutting systems. Automated spreading ensures consistent fabric tension (preventing distortion in the finished garment) and optimal lay planning to minimise waste. Our factories maintain a cutting waste rate of 6.2% — well below the industry average of 15%.

Cut panels are bundled and barcoded in sequential work tickets that travel with the garment through all subsequent production stages, maintaining end-to-end traceability. Hand-cutting is used only for specialist fabrics like plaid matching and embroidered panels.

Lectra Investronica Cutters 6.2% Cutting Waste Rate Automated Spreading Barcode Work Tickets Plaid Matching by Hand
6

Sewing & Assembly

Assembly is carried out by skilled machinists in our three factories. Braga specialises in knitwear and jersey garments; Jaipur handles woven shirts, blouses, and lightweight dresses; Cusco focuses on outerwear and wool-blend garments. Machinists are each skilled on 3–5 machine types including overlock (Pegasus), flatlock (Union Special), chainstitch (Yamato), and lockstitch (Juki).

All factories operate a modular production system (small batch cells of 8–12 workers) rather than the conventional line system — this results in shorter cycle times, lower in-process inventory, and greater skill development per worker. Average sewing experience per machinist: 9.4 years.

Pegasus Overlock Machines Juki Lockstitch Machines Union Special Flatlock Modular Production Cells 9.4yr Avg Machinist Experience 3 Specialist Factories
7

Quality Control & Inspection

Quality control operates at four checkpoints: in-line inspection (every 2 hours per cell), end-of-line 100% visual check, measurement audit (AQL 2.5 on critical dimensions), and pre-shipment inspection. Our QC team of 42 inspectors uses the ASTM D3512 pilling standard, AATCC 61 washing fastness, and ISO 105-B02 light fastness protocols.

Defect rates are tracked per cell, per machinist, and per machine in real time on our factory floor dashboards. Current factory-average defect rate: 0.8% (industry benchmark 3–5%). Garments failing inspection are either reworked or repurposed as fabric offcuts for our zero-waste accessories line.

AQL 2.5 Measurement Audit AATCC 61 Wash Fastness ISO 105-B02 Light Fastness 42 QC Inspectors 0.8% Defect Rate Real-Time Defect Dashboard
8

Finishing, Labelling & Packaging

Finished garments are steamed, folded, and packaged using 100% recycled kraft paper tissue (FSC-certified) and polybags made from 80% recycled polyethylene. Our hangtags are printed on seed paper embedded with wildflower seeds — customers are encouraged to plant them. Care labels use PVC-free woven satin and are printed with water-based inks.

Outer shipping cartons use 100% recycled corrugated board, sealed with water-activated tape (no plastic tape). Each garment's unique QR code links to its full production journey — fibre origin, factory, machinist team, and all test results.

FSC-Certified Kraft Paper 80% Recycled Polybags Seed Paper Hangtags Water-Based Ink Labels QR Production Journey 100% Recycled Cartons

Our Certifications

Independent certification from the world's most rigorous textile standards bodies.

🌿

GOTS — Global Organic Textile Standard

The world's leading processing standard for organic fibres. Covers ecological and social criteria along the entire textile supply chain. Certificate #GOTS-EU-2021-0847.

✅

OEKO-TEX STANDARD 100

Every fabric and component (buttons, zips, labels) tested for 100+ harmful substances. Products certified at Product Class I (suitable for babies). Certificate #22.HUS.89421.

🏭

ISO 9001:2015 — Quality Management

All three factories certified to ISO 9001:2015. Scope covers garment design, production, and distribution. Last audit: March 2025. Next audit: March 2026.

⚡

ISO 14001:2015 — Environmental Management

Environmental management system covering energy use, water consumption, waste management, and chemical handling at all manufacturing sites.

🤝

Fairtrade Textile Standard

Certified under the Fairtrade Textile Standard for our cotton supply chain. Covers fair prices, fair labour conditions, and community development premiums to farming cooperatives.

🐑

Responsible Wool Standard (RWS)

All wool products carry RWS certification, guaranteeing wool is sourced from farms with a progressive approach to managing their land and the animals in their care.
